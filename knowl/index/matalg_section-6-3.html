We have the following rules for matrix multiplication. Suppose that \(A\text{,}\) \(B\text{,}\) \(C\) are matrices of the correct sizes so that the following make sense. Let \(\alpha\) denote a scalar (number). Then
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} A(BC) & = (AB)C & & \text{(associative law)} , \\ A(B+C) & = AB + AC & & \text{(distributive law)} , \\ (B+C)A & = BA + CA & & \text{(distributive law)} , \\ \alpha(AB) & = (\alpha A)B = A(\alpha B) , & & \\ IA & = A = AI & & \text{(identity)}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
