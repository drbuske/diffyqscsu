The method of separation of variables is to try to find solutions that are products of functions of one variable. For the heat equation, we try to find solutions of the form
\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = X(x)T(t) . \end{equation*}
That the desired solution we are looking for is of this form is too much to hope for. What is perfectly reasonable to ask, however, is to find enough ‚Äúbuilding-block‚Äù solutions of the form \(u(x,t) = X(x)T(t)\) using this procedure so that the desired solution to the PDE is somehow constructed from these building blocks by the use of superposition.
