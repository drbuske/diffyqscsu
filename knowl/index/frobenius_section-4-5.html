Theorem 4.3.1. Method of Frobenius.

Suppose that
\begin{equation} p(x) y'' + q(x) y' + r(x) y = 0\tag{4.3} \end{equation}
has a regular singular point at \(x=0\text{,}\) then there exists at least one solution of the form
\begin{equation*} y = x^r \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k x^k . \end{equation*}
A solution of this form is called a Frobenius-type solution.
