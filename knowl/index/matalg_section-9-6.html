We say a matrix \(A\) is symmetric if \(A = A^T\text{.}\) For example,
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & 5 \\ 3 & 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
is a symmetric matrix. Notice that a symmetric matrix is always square, that is, \(n \times n\text{.}\) Symmetric matrices have many nice properties
