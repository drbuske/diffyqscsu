We will mostly consider the linear systems. The example above is a so-called linear first order system. It is linear as none of the dependent variables or their derivatives appear in nonlinear functions or with powers higher than one (\(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(x'\) and \(y'\text{,}\) constants, and functions of \(t\) can appear, but not \(xy\) or \({(y')}^2\) or \(x^3\)). Another, more complicated, example of a linear system is
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} y_1'' &= e^t y_1' + t^2 y_1 + 5 y_2 + \sin(t), \\ y_2'' &= t y_1'-y_2' + 2 y_1 + \cos(t). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
