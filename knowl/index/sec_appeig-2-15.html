There are other boundary conditions than just hinged ends. There are three basic possibilities: hinged, free, or fixed. Let us consider the end at \(x=0\text{.}\) For the other end, it is the same idea. If the end is hinged, then
\begin{equation*} u(0,t) = u_{xx}(0,t) = 0 . \end{equation*}
If the end is free, that is, it is just floating in air, then
\begin{equation*} u_{xx}(0,t) = u_{xxx}(0,t) = 0 . \end{equation*}
And finally, if the end is clamped or fixed, for example it is welded to a wall, then
\begin{equation*} u(0,t) = u_{x}(0,t) = 0 . \end{equation*}
