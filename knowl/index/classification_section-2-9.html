A linear equation may further be called homogeneous if all terms depend on the dependent variable. That is, if no term is a function of the independent variables alone. Otherwise, the equation is called nonhomogeneous or inhomogeneous. For example, the exponential growth equation, the wave equation, or the transport equation above are homogeneous. The mechanical vibrations equation above is nonhomogeneous as long as \(f(t)\) is not the zero function.
