    Section Overview

    This section introduces autonomous differential equations, equations of the form \(x' = f(x)\) whose rate of change depends only on the dependent variable and not on the indpendent variable (which is often time \(t\)). Because of this simplification, autonomous equations allow powerful qualitative analysis: identifying equilibrium (constant) solutions, determining critical points where \(f(x)=0\text{,}\) and analyzing the stability of these equilibria by observing how nearby solutions behave. Using examples such as Newtonâ€™s law of cooling and the logistic population model, the section shows how slope fields reveal longâ€‘term behaviorâ€”such as convergence to a stable equilibrium or divergence from an unstable oneâ€”even without solving the ODE explicitly.
