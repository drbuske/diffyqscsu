    Section Overview

    In this section, we will learn how to recognize, interpret, and solve secondâ€‘order linear ordinary differential equations, which take the general form \(A(x) y'' + B(x) y' + C(x) y = F(x)\text{.}\) After rewriting such equations in the standardized form \(y'' + p(x) y' + q(x) y = f(x)\text{,}\) we will explore the foundational structure of linear ODEs, including the distinction between homogeneous and nonhomogeneous equations and the crucial fact that no powers or nonlinear functions of \(y\text{,}\) \(y'\text{,}\) or \(y''\) appear. A major focus is understanding the principle of superposition, which allows solutions of homogeneous equations to be combined into new solutions, and learning how this principle, along with existence and uniqueness results, gives linear ODEs a powerful and predictable solution theory. We will also be introduced to the operator viewpoint \(L y = y'' + p y' + q y\text{,}\) which provides an elegant framework for understanding why linear equations behave so systematically.
