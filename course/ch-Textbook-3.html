    Section Overview

    This section shows how certain first-order differential equations can be solved simply by antidifferentiating. When the equation has the form \(y' = f(x)\text{,}\) its general solution is just an antiderivative of \(f(x)\) plus a constant. The text also clarifies the difference between definite and indefinite integrals and explains how initial conditions lead to a definiteâ€‘integral formula for the solution.
